\chapter*{Introduction}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Introduction}

Black holes are usually introduced through highly symmetric exact solutions of
Einstein's equations. The Schwarzschild and Reissner--Nordstr\"om geometries are
central examples. Their symmetry makes the geodesic motion of test particles
regular: enough conserved quantities exist to reduce the dynamics to an
integrable problem. This is not only mathematically convenient, but also
physically useful, because such solutions give the reference space-times around
which more complicated astrophysical models can be built.

Astrophysical black holes, however, are not isolated ideal bodies. They are
surrounded by accreting matter, electromagnetic fields, stars, or other compact
objects. Even a simplified model of additional matter in the form of an
axisymmetric disc or ring changes the geometry experienced by a test particle.
Such perturbations can break the integrability of the isolated black-hole
problem and produce chaotic geodesic dynamics. This possibility has been studied
in a sequence of works on black holes with external discs or rings, using
Poincar\'e sections, analytic criteria and the Melnikov method
\cite{Semerak2016,Witzany2015,PolcarThesis2018,Polcar2019,PolcarSemerak2019}.

The astrophysical motivation is therefore not that the thin rings used in this
thesis are literal models of accretion flows. They are controlled idealizations
of external gravitating matter. Their advantage is that they keep the problem
stationary and axially symmetric while still perturbing the black-hole field
strongly enough to test how regular motion is destroyed. In this setting, chaos
is relevant because it limits final-state predictability: two nearby initial
conditions can lead to different outcomes, such as long-lived bounded motion or
termination near the black hole or the ring. Basin maps and fractal dimensions
provide a way to quantify this sensitivity.

This thesis follows the work of David Sychrovsky~\cite{Sychrovsky2020}, who
studied basin maps and basin-boundary dimensions for a Schwarzschild black hole
perturbed by external Weyl sources. We first reproduce selected results for the
Schwarzschild--Bach--Weyl system, using a different codebase for trajectory
integration and basin-map construction. The thesis repository is available at
\url{https://github.com/DJopek/chaos}. We then apply the same final-state and
uncertainty-exponent methods to the
Reissner--Nordstr\"om--Majumdar--Papapetrou system, where previous work connects
the onset of chaos to homoclinic tangles detected by the Melnikov method
\cite{PolcarSemerak2019}.
